# Source: Immigration & Checkpoints Authority (Singapore)

**Type**: Case study / integrated agency model
**Confidence**: medium (Wikipedia summary; primary ICA documentation would be higher confidence)

## Key Claims

- ICA formed 1 April 2003 by merging Singapore Immigration & Registration with Customs & Excise Department
- Governing acts: Immigration Act, Passports Act, National Registration Act — Singapore operates immigration, passport and civil registration under a single agency
- HQ: 2 Crawford Street, Singapore 207218

## Significance for Consular Service Management

Demonstrates the **consolidated "one-stop" agency model** for travel-document and identity workflows. Relevant because [[CV-Syamil-Protkons|PROTKONS]] currently spans passport, legalisir, and citizen registration across multiple roles (Loket, Verifikasi, HS, Kasir) — but those roles are internal to one embassy, not statutory consolidation across passport + immigration + civil registration.

## Pattern

```
Integrated Agency Model (Singapore ICA):
  Immigration Act → Passport Act → National Registration Act
    → Single counter for: visa, passport, citizen ID, civil registration

PROTKONS Current Model:
  KBRI KL only → passport + legalisir + consular certificates
    → Separate from Dirjen Imigrasi (passport issuance)
    → Separate from Dukcapil (civil registration)
```

Indonesia does NOT have an ICA-equivalent. KBRI/KJRI consular services run in parallel with Dirjen Imigrasi (passports) and Dukcapil (civil registration) rather than being unified. This is a structural gap relative to Singapore.
