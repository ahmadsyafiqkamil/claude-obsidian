It's time for me to update and write an article about which coins to avoid when doing SOL sided DLMM positions. These are just my methods of screening coins which works for me. Perhaps there are other better ways that I have not figured out yet. ===================================================== **1\. Dexscreener coins without images or socials** ✅ If the coin has no logo - skip it ❌ If the coin has no social links - skip it ❌ However scammers/ruggers are working hard these days so the coins mostly have social links. Then we continue screening the coin of interest. ===================================================== **2\. What coin is this?** There are many genres of coins these days so the obvious no no for me are as follows as they tend to rug quickly even though they have volume. - **Trump/Elon/Baron coins** (Every week we will get their coins, all crap) ❌ Any coin with faces of political people is a no no.

![Image](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/G_PdOjlbEAIzAHt?format=png&name=large)

\- **Celebrity coins** (Remember Kanye) ❌ - **Justice** for someone/some animal (Remember Iryna) ❌ Recently scammers like to use other people's/animal's reputations to scam/rug. - **Animal celebrities** (Tiktok animals) ❌ - **Dev coins (BAGS coins)** ❌ For BAGS coins, if it is a DEV coin, look up the socials first, if the Dev is helping the community then it can be a good coin, but if the Dev has no signs of being there that is a red flag. The problem with these coins mentioned above is that we don't know when the Dev decides to cash in and start dumping the charts. Usually when they start to dump the chart becomes a slow rug, no bounce is seen until the chart dumps down over -80% in 24h. So to be profitable, throw away that greed and just ignore these coins for good mental health. ===================================================== 3**. Total fees on GMGN** ✅ The total fees indicator on GMGN can tell us a lot about a coin's tokenomics. It tells us how organic the buying and selling are. A low number usually indicates not much interest in the coin, and most of the time it is due to the metrics. Could be high top10 holders, high bundling/phishing or anything that makes people not want to trade it. Generally for me if the number is below 20, it is a scam coin and will rug to zero at any possible time. While if the number is above 50 it tends to be "safe" for DLMM as it tends to chop around giving us fees. Between 20-50 is maybe yes and no, have to look at the charts. However a high number does not mean the coin is totally safe to DLMM forever. It may be cos of initial mass hype. We still have to check other stuffs.

![Image](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/G_O8PAkbIAASVNA?format=jpg&name=large)

\===================================================== 4**. Check these metrics on GMGN** - Top 10 - above 30% is a red flag 🟥 - Dev - Above 5% is a red flag, but generally if Dev has even 1% supply is a red flag cos they can dump anytime. 🟥 - Insiders - Above 0% is a red flag 🟥 - Phishing - Above 30% is a red flag 🟥 - Bundling - For me personally above 60% is a red flag 🟥as bundling may be due to alpha groups making calls and hundreds of members buying at the same time, causing them to be labeled as bundled - Initial Liquidity not Burnt is also a red flag 🟥 - If the Dev has a history of rugging, that is also a red flag s the coin may not pump much due to constant dumps from the Dev's other wallets.

![Image](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/G_PC0zma0AAfwqH?format=png&name=large)

\====================================================== **5\. Vamped coins (On GMGN you will see a red icon like vampire fangs)**

![Image](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HBuspeqb0AE0Nsp?format=jpg&name=large)

The idea of Vampire coins is that it is to extract liquidity from the main runner, people that missed out on the main runner might think that the Vamped coin is the beta play that can make the same pump like the first coin. But most of the time it is just pure extraction, very quickly dumped. Always avoid when you see one. Note that sometimes GMGN is late in updating this Vamp coin status. So we will have to check back when we see price not behaving normally. ======================================================= **6\. CTO coins (Community Takeover Coins)**

![Image](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HBut5cXaMAEf0p0?format=png&name=large)

CTO coins used to be a good thing, a coin rugged and the community bands together to make it pump even higher than the previous dev. However recently bad people have used this feature to extract even more from the bagholders. So instead of working to pump the coin back to ATH, bad people apply to CTO the coin and become the new devs who gets the creator fees. So the new dev can start earning creator fees and keep dumping on the chart. All they need to do is to keep hyping up the project on X. Bagholders who are in pain will think that the new dev wants to pump the coin back up so they buy more hoping to earn bigger when the coin goes back up. But however the new devs does not pump it and keep getting creator fees from people buying and selling the coin. In the end only the new devs earn while the chart keeps dumping. So avoid CTO coins from now on. ======================================================== **7\. Virus Clusters (Bubblemaps)** When you see such clusters on bubblemaps, just avoid it. I call them virus clusters cos they look like an adenovirus. This means that insiders have already bought a big bag and use multiple wallets to hold and can dump the charts in one click. Just avoid such coins to prevent regret.

![Image](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HBuvMtcbgAA34QQ?format=png&name=large)

\======================================================== 8. Pumpfun Offchain Coins

![Image](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HIkJB9-bwAAZtvX?format=png&name=large)

I took note of this metric and this is the second time I see it and the same thing happened, the coin just pumps and slow rugs without any meaningful bounce until it has rugged over -90%. So what this means is just that the creator and the minter are different wallets. The minter is the first buyer. The creator created the coin but noone bought, after some time the rugger(first buyer) used this coin for crime. It is very similar to CTO coin where a community takes over to extract fees. This time they use a dead coin to do crime. Possibly to just avoid getting found. So this offchain metric goes into my blacklist, cos I seen it happen 2 times and with the same results. ======================================================== Once the coin of interest clears all these metrics. We can look at the charts in the next article. Usually in 1 day, we would be able to filter out 99% of the coins launched, leaving only 1-2 coins for us to DLMM.
